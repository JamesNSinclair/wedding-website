After 10 years together (many of which James had to pretend he likes clubs and none which Caz even pretended to like Pokemon), Carolyn and James are finally getting married.

Travel

Travel to Cornwall by train

Contact Great Western Railway for train times and fare information on 0345 700 0125 (7am – 10pm every day)

High speed train services run frequently from London Paddington station including the Night Riviera Sleeper Service to Penzance. There are also direct trains daily from Bath and Bristol to Cornwall and its branch lines. More info from: nationalrail.co.uk

CrossCountry operate trains from the midlands, the north of England and Scotland.

The First Group co-ordinate rail and bus services from stations. Park-and-ride schemes run in season at Liskeard (for Looe) and Lelant Saltings (for St. Ives).

Travel

Travel to Cornwall by road

It takes less than five hours to get from London to the heart of Cornwall by either train or car. Drive along the M4 motorway from London, or M6 from Manchester, and then the M5 to Exeter (or take the M3 and the A303 across Salisbury Plain) and finally either the A30, that is mostly dual carriageway, or the A38 through Plymouth and Saltash into South East Cornwall. Alternatively if you enter Cornwall from North Devon, there is the scenic Atlantic Highway A39 through Bude which you can join from Junction 27 on the M5. Due to the high volume of traffic on roads during the summer months, travelling to Cornwall overnight or outside peak rush hours is recommended to avoid long delays.

Or, you know, just use a satnav...

Travel to Cornwall by plane

You can fly to Cornwall from airports around the UK, Ireland, Germany and Spain. Cornwall Airport Newquay currently has year round daily flights from London Gatwick and Manchester with additional seasonal routes (between March and October) from Aberdeen, Belfast, Birmingham, Doncaster-Sheffield, Edinburgh, London Stansted, and Newcastle (Flybe); Dublin (Aer Lingus); Alicante and Frankfurt-Hahn (Ryanair); and Dusseldorf (Eurowings) - provided they are still operating after lockdown.
